# Scouting

**Scouting** is the deliberate act of gathering information about your opponent's strategy early in the game.
Knowing what race, tech path, and unit composition your opponent is building lets you make informed decisions
rather than guessing — and in WC3, incorrect guesses often cost games.

## Why It Matters

WC3 has a small number of viable strategies per matchup, but each has a specific counter.
Without scouting, you must play a generic response and hope it works. With scouting, you play
a targeted counter that shifts the probability of winning significantly in your favour.

## How to Apply

Send your starting worker (or a cheap unit) toward your opponent's base within the first 30–60 seconds.
Your goal is to identify: (1) what race they are, (2) what building they placed first, and (3) whether
they are expanding early or teching immediately.

In your next five games, write down what you saw on your first scout and whether your response matched it.
Even a partial scouting run — seeing just one building — gives you actionable information. Start the habit
before worrying about perfecting the execution.
