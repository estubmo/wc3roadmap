# Expansion Timing

**Expansion timing** is the decision of when to build a secondary base to increase your resource income.
Expanding too early leaves you vulnerable with insufficient army to defend; expanding too late concedes
an economic lead to an opponent who expanded at the right moment.

## Why It Matters

A well-timed expansion doubles your income over the course of the mid-game, funding a technology
and army advantage that becomes decisive by the late game. The key insight is that the timing window
is situational — it depends on your opponent's aggression, your army strength, and your gold income.
There is no universal "expand at 5 minutes"; there is only "expand when your army can hold it."

## How to Apply

Identify three conditions that must be true before you expand: (1) your army can defend the expansion
from your opponent's current force, (2) you are not supply-stalled, and (3) your gold is high enough
that the expansion pays off before the game ends. Check these three conditions in your next replay
at the moment you expanded — or identify why you never expanded. The goal is conscious timing,
not defaulting to a fixed minute mark.
