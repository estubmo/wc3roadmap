# Orc Opening Build Order

The **standard Orc opening** prioritises tempo: continuous Peon production out of the Great Hall, a
Barracks placed as early as gold allows, and a first Grunt queued the instant the Barracks completes. Orc's
identity is built around reaching this first combat unit faster than the opponent expects.

## Why It Matters

Orc's early aggression options — camp pressure, harassment, or a straight rush — only exist if the first
Grunt exists on time. Every second of delay in Peon production or Barracks placement is a second Orc's
tempo advantage evaporates, and Orc has less to fall back on economically than a race that plays a slower,
more defensive opening. A fast, consistent opener is what makes the rest of Orc's kit (Grunt-Raider timing
pushes, early Burrow harass) viable at all.

## How to Apply

Target: your **first Grunt should complete before the 1:40 mark**. Drill this in isolation — for your
next ten games, focus exclusively on an unbroken Peon queue and Barracks timing for the first 100 seconds,
ignoring scouting and matchup adjustments entirely. A first Grunt arriving after 1:40 almost always traces
back to a delayed Barracks, not slow production afterward. Upload the replay and mastery advances
automatically once your parsed timing clears the threshold.
