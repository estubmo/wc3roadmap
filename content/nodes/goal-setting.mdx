# Goal Setting

**Goal setting** is choosing one specific, measurable thing to improve per practice session instead of
playing to "get better" in general. A vague intention produces vague practice; a precise target produces
focused reps you can actually evaluate.

## Why It Matters

Decades of goal-setting research find that specific, measurable, moderately hard goals reliably beat "do
your best." In WC3 terms, a session aimed at "improve" gives your brain nothing to check against, so you
drift back into your habits. A session aimed at "keep continuous worker production for the first three
minutes" is checkable in the replay — you either did it or you didn't — which is what makes practice add up.

## How to Apply

Before your next session, write down one measurable goal tied to a single node — a timing, a mechanic, a
habit — and only that. Play your games trying to hit it, ignore everything else you might also fix, then
check the replay against the goal. Next session, either raise the bar or pick the next skill. This pairs
directly with replay review and spaced practice: the goal is what those two are measuring.
