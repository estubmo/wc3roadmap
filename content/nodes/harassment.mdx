# Harassment

**Harassment** is the practice of sending a small number of fast or flying units to attack your opponent's
workers or buildings while the main fight is elsewhere. A successful harassment kills workers or delays
production without cost. An unsuccessful harassment loses your harassing units for minimal opponent
economic damage.

## Why It Matters

Worker kills are among the highest-value actions in WC3. Each dead worker is permanent lost income.
Even killing two or three workers during a window when your opponent is distracted by a creep camp
or a secondary engagement can create an economic lead that persists through the entire game.

## How to Apply

Before attempting worker harassment, master micro-focus-fire with your main army. Harassment requires
you to micro two things simultaneously — your main army and your harassing unit. Begin with the
simplest possible form: send a flying unit (Hippogriff, Gargoyle, or equivalent) to your opponent's
gold mine while your army creeps. Do not attack-move the base — right-click one worker at a time
and retreat when your harassing unit drops below 50% HP. One successful kill per harassment is a
profitable exchange.
