# Tilt and Nerves

**Tilt and nerves** is managing the emotional side of competition: the frustration after a loss that makes
your next game worse, and the pressure in a close game that makes you fumble basics you never miss in
practice. Composure is a trainable skill, not a fixed trait.

## Why It Matters

Pressure consumes working memory — the same limited resource your macro and micro need — which is why tense
games produce errors you would never make in a practice match: a missed worker, a mistimed engagement, a
fumbled hotkey. And tilt after a loss carries the last game's mistakes into the next one. Left unmanaged,
both quietly cap how much your mechanical skill can actually express.

## How to Apply

Build a between-games reset: after every game, win or lose, take a short fixed routine — stand up, breathe,
one sentence on what to do next game — before you requeue. Never immediately re-queue on tilt. In a close
game, when you feel the pressure spike, deliberately return to your scan cycle and worker queue; anchoring
on a trained habit is what stops pressure from hijacking your attention. Treat composure as reps, like any
other skill here.
