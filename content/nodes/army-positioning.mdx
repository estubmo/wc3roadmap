# Army Positioning

**Army positioning** is the intentional placement of your units before and during a fight to maximise
the number of your units dealing damage while minimising the number of your opponent's units that can
attack you simultaneously. Formation shapes a fight before a single ability is cast.

## Why It Matters

WC3 fights are typically decided in the first few seconds of contact. An army that approaches in a concave
arc (wrapping around the enemy) deals more damage per second than a clumped column that can only attack
with its front row. Positioning turns a numerically equal fight into a numerical advantage.

## How to Apply

Before your next engagement, take a half-second to spread your army into a wide arc facing your opponent.
Do not move into the fight in a single clump — arrive from the side that forces your opponent's units to
rotate or reposition. As a drill: in your next five fights, consciously ask "Am I in a concave or convex
formation?" before engaging. Building this pause habit is worth more than the half-second of fight time it costs.
