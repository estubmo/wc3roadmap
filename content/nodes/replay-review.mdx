# Replay Review

**Replay review** is the practice of re-watching your own games to find the specific decisions that
decided the outcome. It is the single cheapest way to improve, because the data you need — every
choice you made — is already recorded.

## Why It Matters

Most games are not lost at the moment they end; they are lost several minutes earlier, at a decision
you did not notice was a mistake. Playing more games without review repeats those mistakes. Deliberate
practice research shows that improvement tracks the quality of feedback, not the quantity of play — and
a replay is a perfect, free feedback source you already own.

## How to Apply

After your next loss, watch **only that one replay** and find a single moment where you would now act
differently — a late expansion, a fight taken on bad ground, a scout you skipped. Write that one lesson
down in a sentence. Do not review the whole game; do not review five games. One replay, one decision,
one sentence. Repeat after each session and you build a personal list of your recurring mistakes, which
is far more useful than any generic tip list.
