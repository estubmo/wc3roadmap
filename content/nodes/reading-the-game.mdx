# Reading the Game

**Reading the game** is recognising the current situation as a familiar pattern — this composition, this
map position, this tempo — and knowing the trained response before you have to think it through.

## Why It Matters

Beginners process the screen piece by piece: this unit, that building, that Hero. Experts see groups —
"a mass-Ghoul push with no detection" — and the correct answer arrives almost instantly. Classic
cognition research on chess found exactly this: expertise is not faster raw thinking, it is perceiving
meaningful chunks instead of isolated parts. The same shift is what separates reacting in time from
reacting too late.

## How to Apply

Pause a replay the instant your opponent's army becomes visible and, before un-pausing, predict their
next move out loud: where they will attack, what they are teching to, what they lack. Then watch what
actually happens. Over many repetitions you are training your pattern library — you are not memorising
one game, you are learning to recognise the *type* of situation so the response is ready next time.
