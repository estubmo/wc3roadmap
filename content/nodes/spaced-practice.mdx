# Spaced Practice

**Spaced practice** is spreading your training across many short sessions instead of cramming it into
one long one. The same number of hours produces more durable skill when the hours are distributed.

## Why It Matters

A four-hour ladder binge feels productive, but fatigue flattens the second half and little of it sticks.
Decades of retention research show the opposite schedule works better: four one-hour sessions on four
days retain more than one four-hour session. Your build orders and hotkey habits consolidate between
sessions, not during a single exhausting one.

## How to Apply

Take whatever you practise this week and cut it into smaller pieces across more days. Instead of one
long session, do twenty focused minutes on a single build order today, then again tomorrow, then the day
after. Sleep between sessions is part of the method — it is when the motor patterns consolidate. Track
consistency of *timing* across days rather than raw games played.
